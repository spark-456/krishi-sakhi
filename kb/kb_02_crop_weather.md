# KB-02: Crop and Weather-Based Agricultural Recommendations — Telangana
## Region: Telangana | Seasons: Kharif, Rabi, Zaid

---

## SECTION 1: Telangana Agro-Climatic Zones

Telangana spans three distinct agro-climatic zones. Crop suitability varies significantly by zone.

### 1.1 Zone Definitions

| Zone | Districts | Rainfall (mm/year) | Soil Types | Key Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Northern Telangana Zone (NTZ) | Adilabad, Mancherial, Nirmal, Kumuram Bheem, Jayashankar Bhupalpally | 900–1200 | Red loam, Black cotton | High rainfall; tribal areas; sorghum, cotton belt |
| Central Telangana Zone (CTZ) | Warangal, Hanamkonda, Karimnagar, Peddapalli, Rajanna Sircilla, Jangaon | 700–1000 | Black cotton, deep red | Major cotton zone; paddy in canal commands |
| Southern Telangana Zone (STZ) | Hyderabad, Rangareddy, Nalgonda, Suryapet, Yadadri, Sangareddy, Medak, Siddipet | 600–900 | Red, black medium | Mixed farming; groundnut, sunflower, horticulture |
| Scarce Rainfall Zone (SRZ) | Mahbubnagar, Nagarkurnool, Wanaparthy, Gadwal, Jogulamba | 500–700 | Red gravelly, black shallow | Dryland farming dominant; groundnut, jowar, castor |

- Source: PJTSAU (Professor Jayashankar Telangana State Agricultural University) Agro-climatic Zone Classification; Telangana State Agriculture Department Zone Maps

### 2.2 Rainfall-Based Sowing Advisories — Kharif

**Trigger for Sowing Onset:**
- Minimum 25 mm cumulative rainfall over 2–3 consecutive days signals reliable sowing window.
- Soil temperature > 25°C at 5 cm depth confirms germination conditions.
- Do not sow dryland crops if forecast shows dry spell > 10 days within 2 weeks of sowing.

| Situation | Advisory |
|---|---|
| Cumulative Kharif rainfall deficit > 20% by end of June | Shift to short-duration varieties (e.g., Paddy: MTU-1010 instead of BPT-5204; Groundnut: K-6 instead of TMV-2) |
| Excessive rainfall (> 150 mm in a week) | Delay paddy transplanting by 1–2 weeks; ensure proper drainage; expect increased blast risk |
| Rainfall < 40 mm in 3rd week of June | Postpone sowing; hold seed; irrigate nursery beds if irrigation available |
| La Nina year (above normal rainfall predicted) | Anticipate waterlogging in black cotton areas; add drainage channels; shift some cotton area to raised bed |
| El Nino year (below normal rainfall predicted) | Prioritize drought-tolerant varieties; reduce area under water-intensive paddy; expand groundnut |

- Source: IMD Telangana Seasonal Forecast; ICAR-CRIDA Contingency Crop Planning; PJTSAU Kharif Advisory Bulletins

## SECTION 4: Zaid / Summer Season (March–June)

Limited cropping in Telangana due to high temperatures (35–45°C). Feasible only in canal/reservoir command areas.

| Crop | Sowing | Harvest | Zone | Remarks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Groundnut | Mar 1 – Mar 31 | Jun | STZ irrigated | Irrigation mandatory; aphid pressure high |
| Maize | Mar 1 – Mar 15 | Jun | CTZ canal | Heat stress after May 1; early varieties |
| Moong (Green Gram) | Mar 1 – Apr 15 | May–Jun | All irrigated | Short duration 60–65 days; catch crop |
| Watermelon | Jan – Feb | Apr–May | SRZ, STZ | Sandy loam; high returns |
| Vegetables (Okra, Bitter Gourd) | Feb – Mar | May | Peri-urban areas | Night temperature dropping critical |

## SECTION 5: Weather-Based Decision Rules

### 5.1 Temperature-Based Crop Management

| Condition | Crop | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Temp > 38°C during paddy flowering | Paddy | Risk of spikelet sterility; irrigation on alternate days to reduce canopy temp |
| Temp < 15°C during paddy grain filling | Paddy (Rabi) | Cold damage risk; drain water to warm soil; apply potassium |
| Temp > 40°C during cotton boll formation | Cotton | Increase irrigation frequency; apply borax 0.5% foliar spray |
| Temp < 12°C at night during chickpea flowering | Chickpea | Terminal cold damage; harvest may advance; no intervention |
| Temp > 35°C during maize silking | Maize | Irrigation on silking day critical; heat stress reduces set |

### 5.2 Humidity-Based Disease Alerts

| Condition | Crop | Expected Disease | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| RH > 90% + temp 20–28°C for 3+ days | Paddy | Blast (Pyricularia oryzae) | Spray Tricyclazole 75 WP @ 0.6 g/L preventively |
| RH > 85% + temp 25–30°C | Tomato | Late blight (Phytophthora infestans) | Spray Metalaxyl + Mancozeb immediately |
| RH > 80% + moderate temp (20–25°C) | Chilli | Anthracnose, die-back | Spray Copper Oxychloride + Carbendazim |
| RH > 90% + standing water | Cotton | Root rot, Fusarium wilt | Improve drainage; Carbendazim drench |
| Prolonged dry spell (RH < 40%) | Cotton | Spider mite outbreak | Spray Spiromesifen or Abamectin; irrigate |

### 5.3 Wind-Based Advisories

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Wind speed > 20 km/hr | Do not spray pesticides; drift damage to crops and health risk |
| Wind speed > 30 km/hr before storm | Remove standing crop water in paddy fields to prevent lodging |
| Prevailing wind from disease-affected neighboring fields | Apply preventive fungicide spray border rows first |

### 5.4 Rainfall-Based Field Management

| Rainfall Event | Crop | Action |
|---|---|---|
| > 100 mm in 24 hours | Paddy | Open drainage; check for blast after waterlogging ends |
| > 100 mm in 24 hours | Cotton | Drain furrows; apply Thiram or Captan if root zone exposed |
| Dry spell > 3 weeks during Kharif | Groundnut | Irrigate @ 40 mm; spray Kaolin 5% to reduce heat stress |
| Dry spell > 2 weeks during cotton fruiting | Cotton | Irrigation mandatory; flower and boll drop starts at -1.5 MPa soil moisture |
| Unseasonal rain during maturity/pre-harvest | Groundnut, Chickpea | Harvest immediately; risk of aflatoxin (groundnut) and Botrytis (chickpea) |

- Source: IMD Telangana Weather Data; ICAR-CRIDA Contingency Planning; PJTSAU Weather-Based Agro-Advisories; APSDPS Advisories

## SECTION 6: Drought Contingency — Telangana

### 6.1 Crop Substitution Under Rainfall Deficit

| Original Crop | Substitute Crop | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Paddy (transplanted) | Short-duration paddy (DRR Dhan 42) or Maize | Reduces water demand by 40% |
| Cotton (full season) | Groundnut or Castor | Lower water requirement; shorter duration |
| Maize | Sorghum or Pearl Millet | C4 crops with better WUE |
| Chickpea (late sowing) | Lentil or Peas | Lower temperature requirement |

### 6.2 Contingency Varieties for Delayed Onset

| Crop | Normal Variety | Contingency (Delayed Sowing by 2–4 weeks) |
|---|---|---|
| Paddy | BPT-5204 (145 d) | MTU-1010 (130 d) or DRR Dhan 42 (110 d) |
| Cotton | Full-season Bt Hybrid | Short-duration Bt (Ankur-2534) |
| Groundnut | K-6 (105 d) | JL-24 (90 d) |
| Maize | DHM-117 (95 d) | 30B07 or local 90-day hybrid |
| Sorghum | CSV-15 (105 d) | CSV-23 (90 d) |

- Source: ICAR-CRIDA Contingency Crop Plans for Telangana (District-wise); PJTSAU Drought Management Manual
