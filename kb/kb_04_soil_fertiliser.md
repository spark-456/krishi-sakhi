# KB-04: Soil Health and Fertiliser Management — Telangana
## Region: Telangana | All Major Crops

---

## SECTION 1: Soil Types in Telangana

### 1.1 Major Soil Groups

| Soil Type | Local Name | Key Properties | Dominant Districts | Best Suited Crops |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Black Cotton Soil (Vertisol) | Regur | High clay content (50–70%); swell-shrink behavior; high SMBC; poor drainage; deep cracks when dry | Warangal, Karimnagar, Adilabad, Nizamabad | Cotton, sorghum, chickpea, wheat, sunflower |
| Red Loamy Soil | Chalka | Moderate clay; good drainage; moderate fertility; porous | NTZ, CTZ mixed | Maize, groundnut, paddy (irrigated), vegetables |
| Red Sandy Soil | Challa | Low clay; low WHC; low CEC; low organic matter; prone to drought | Mahbubnagar, Nalgonda, SRZ | Groundnut, castor, sorghum, millet |
| Alluvial Soil | — | Rich in nutrients; good structure; high organic matter; riverine | Godavari, Krishna basin areas | Paddy, banana, sugarcane, vegetables |
| Laterite | — | Iron-rich; acidic (pH 5–6); low phosphorus availability; poor |Parts of NTZ tribal areas | Coarse cereals, cashew (if managed) |

### 1.2 Soil pH Distribution — Telangana

| pH Range | Soil Reaction | Districts | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 5.5 | Strongly acidic | Adilabad forested zones | Lime application; 1–2 t/ha agricultural lime |
| 5.5–6.5 | Moderately acidic | NTZ laterite areas | Lime at 0.5 t/ha; adequate P application |
| 6.5–7.5 | Neutral (ideal) | Most alluvial, red loam | Standard fertiliser program |
| 7.5–8.5 | Moderately alkaline | Vertisols, Nalgonda | Gypsum application; Zn sulfate needed |
| > 8.5 | Strongly alkaline | Saline/alkali pockets (Nalgonda) | Gypsum 2–5 t/ha; pyrite; acidifying fertilisers |

- Source: PJTSAU Soil Survey; NBSS&LUP (National Bureau of Soil Survey and Land Use Planning) Telangana Soil Map

## SECTION 2: Nutrient Status — Telangana Soils

### 2.1 Macro-Nutrient Status (Telangana Average)

| Nutrient | Deficient | Sufficient | % Deficient Soils (TG) | Common Symptom |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nitrogen (N) | < 280 kg/ha (KMnO4 method) | > 560 kg/ha | 85–90% | Yellowing (chlorosis) from older leaves; poor growth |
| Phosphorus (P) | < 11 kg/ha (Olsen) | > 22 kg/ha | 65–75% | Purpling of leaves; poor root development |
| Potassium (K) | < 108 kg/ha (NH4OAc) | > 280 kg/ha | 30–40% | Leaf scorching (margin); poor boll/pod fill |

**Note:** Almost all Telangana soils are nitrogen-deficient. Phosphorus deficiency is widespread in red soils. Potassium deficiency is increasing due to high-yielding varieties.

### 2.2 Micro-Nutrient Status

| Micronutrient | Critical Level | % Deficient (TG) | Most Deficient Districts | Crop Most Affected |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Zinc (Zn) | < 0.6 mg/kg (DTPA) | 45–55% | Nalgonda, Medak, Ranga Reddy | Paddy, maize, wheat |
| Iron (Fe) | < 4.5 mg/kg (DTPA) | 15–20% | Alkaline vertisol areas | Groundnut (iron-deficiency chlorosis) |
| Boron (B) | < 0.5 mg/kg (hot water) | 40–50% | Cotton belt | Cotton (hollow heart); sunflower |
| Manganese (Mn) | < 1.0 mg/kg (DTPA) | 10–15% | Alkaline soils | Paddy, wheat |
| Copper (Cu) | < 0.2 mg/kg (DTPA) | 10% | Sandy soils | Rare; maize, wheat |
| Sulphur (S) | < 10 mg/kg (CaCl2) | 35–40% | Light textured soils | Groundnut, oilseeds |

- Source: PJTSAU Soil Health Survey 2019; ICAR-IISS (Indian Institute of Soil Science) Micronutrient Atlas

## SECTION 3: Fertiliser Recommendations by Crop — Telangana

### 3.1 Paddy — Fertiliser Schedule

#### NPK Recommendations (kg/ha)
| Situation | N | P₂O₅ | K₂O |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-yielding (< 4 t/ha) | 80 | 40 | 40 |
| Medium-yielding (4–5 t/ha) | 100 | 50 | 50 |
| High-yielding (> 5 t/ha) | 120 | 60 | 60 |
| HYV transplanted (CTZ, irrigated) | 120 | 60 | 60 |

#### Application Schedule — Paddy (Transplanted, 120 kg N example)
| Timing | N (kg/ha) | P₂O₅ (kg/ha) | K₂O (kg/ha) | Form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basal (at transplanting) | 40 | 60 | 60 | DAP + MOP or SSP + Urea |
| 1st Top Dress (21–25 DAT) | 40 | — | — | Urea |
| 2nd Top Dress (45–50 DAT, PI) | 40 | — | — | Urea |

**Zinc in Paddy:**
- Apply Zinc Sulfate (21% Zn) at 25 kg/ha as basal where DTPA-Zn < 0.6 mg/kg.
- Foliar: Zinc Sulfate 0.5% + Lime 0.25% at tillering and PI stage.

#### Biofertiliser for Paddy
| Biofertiliser | Dose | Method | Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Azospirillum | 3 packets (600 g)/ha | Root dip or soil application | 15–20 kg N saved/ha |
| Blue Green Algae (BGA) | 10 kg/ha | Broadcast in standing water | 25–30 kg N/ha fixation |
| Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria (PSB) | 3 packets/ha | Soil application | 15–25% P efficiency gain |

- Source: PJTSAU Paddy Production Manual; ICAR-CRRI Nutrient Management; Telangana Agriculture Dept Fertiliser Recommendations

### 3.2 Cotton — Fertiliser Schedule

#### NPK Recommendations
| System | N (kg/ha) | P₂O₅ (kg/ha) | K₂O (kg/ha) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rainfed (< 1500 kg/ha) | 60 | 30 | 30 |
| Irrigated (1500–2000 kg/ha) | 100 | 50 | 50 |
| Bt Hybrid, irrigated (> 2000 kg/ha) | 120–150 | 60 | 60 |

#### Application Schedule — Bt Cotton, Irrigated (120 kg N)
| Timing | N | P₂O₅ | K₂O | Form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basal (at sowing) | 30 | 60 | 60 | DAP + MOP |
| 30 DAS | 30 | — | — | Urea |
| 60 DAS (squaring) | 30 | — | — | Urea |
| 90 DAS (boll formation) | 30 | — | 30 | Urea + MOP |

**Micronutrients for Cotton:**
- Boron: 1.5 kg B/ha as Borax (basal) OR 0.2% Borax foliar spray at squaring and boll formation.
- Sulphur: Apply 40 kg/ha single super phosphate (SSP) as P source to supply S simultaneously.
- Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO₄/ha as basal if soil test < 0.6 mg/kg.

### 3.3 Groundnut — Fertiliser Schedule

Groundnut is a legume — N fixation reduces external N need. Excess N suppresses nodulation.

| Stage | N (kg/ha) | P₂O₅ (kg/ha) | K₂O (kg/ha) | Gypsum (kg/ha) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basal | 20 | 50–60 | 40 | — | Starter N only |
| 30 DAS | — | — | — | 500 | Gypsum at flower pegging; critical for pod Ca |
| 60 DAS | — | — | 20 | — | Pod filling; K boosts oil content |

**Gypsum Application — Critical for Groundnut:**
- Apply 500 kg/ha gypsum at pegging stage (30–35 DAS). Top-dress around plants.
- Gypsum supplies calcium directly to developing pods (pod uptake from soil, not translocated from leaves).
- Without gypsum on calcium-deficient red soils: empty pods ("pops"), poor germination of seed.
- Source: ICAR-ICRISAT; PJTSAU Groundnut Manual

**Rhizobium Inoculation:**
- Treat seed with Bradyrhizobium japonicum @ 200 g/10 kg seed before sowing.
- Saves 20–25 kg N/ha; improves nodulation.

### 3.5 Chickpea — Fertiliser Schedule

Chickpea requires minimal N as it fixes its own.

| Stage | N (kg/ha) | P₂O₅ (kg/ha) | K₂O (kg/ha) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basal (at sowing) | 20 | 40–50 | 20 |
| No top dress required | — | — | — |

- Rhizobium inoculation: Mesorhizobium ciceri @ 200 g/10 kg seed. Critical on first-time chickpea fields.
- Boron foliar (0.2% borax) at flower initiation improves pod setting.

### 3.6 Tomato — Fertiliser Schedule (Transplanted)

| Stage (DAT) | N (kg/ha) | P₂O₅ (kg/ha) | K₂O (kg/ha) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basal | 40 | 80 | 80 | FYM 25 t/ha incorporated at land prep |
| 20–25 DAT | 40 | — | — | Urea top dress |
| 40–45 DAT (flowering) | 40 | — | 40 | Urea + MOP |
| 60–70 DAT (fruit set) | 30 | — | 40 | Urea + MOP |
| **Total** | **150** | **80** | **160** | |

- Boron: 0.2% borax foliar at flowering improves fruit set.

## SECTION 4: Soil Health Card (SHC) — How to Use

### 4.1 What is Tested
- pH, EC, OC (Organic Carbon), N, P, K, S, Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu, B

### 4.2 Interpreting SHC and Acting on It

| Parameter | Action if Low | Product | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic Carbon (< 0.5%) | Apply FYM / compost / green manure | FYM | 10–12 t/ha/year |
| N (low) | Increase N fertiliser; add green manure | Urea (46% N) | As per crop recommendation |
| P (low) | Apply SSP or DAP; add PSB | SSP (16% P₂O₅) or DAP | As per crop recommendation |
| K (low) | Apply MOP or SOP | MOP (60% K₂O) | As per crop recommendation |
| Zn (low) | Apply ZnSO₄ | Zinc Sulfate 21% | 25 kg/ha basal |
| B (low) | Apply Borax | Borax (10.5% B) | 1.5 kg/ha basal or 0.2% foliar |
| S (low) | Use SSP as P source | SSP | Supplies 11% S simultaneously |
| pH > 8.5 | Apply gypsum or elemental S | Gypsum | 2–5 t/ha |
| pH < 5.5 | Apply agricultural lime | Agricultural lime | 1–2 t/ha |

- Source: SHC Portal (soilhealth.dac.gov.in); ICAR-IISS Fertiliser Recommendations; PJTSAU Soil Management Guidelines

## SECTION 5: Organic Matter and Soil Biology

### 5.1 Importance of Organic Carbon

| OC Level (%) | Soil Health Status | Effect on Crop |
|---|---|---|
| < 0.5 | Critical — poor | Low nutrient retention; poor structure; high erosion |
| 0.5–0.75 | Poor | Suboptimal; regular FYM application needed |
| 0.75–1.0 | Moderate | Adequate for most crops with supplemental fertiliser |
| > 1.0 | Good | High biological activity; better nutrient cycling |

**Building Organic Matter in Telangana:**
- Rice straw incorporation (instead of burning): +0.1–0.2% OC per season
- Green manuring with Dhaincha (Sesbania) or Sunnhemp: +30–40 kg N/ha; +0.15% OC
- FYM / Vermicompost: 5 t/ha vermicompost = 3 t/ha FYM equivalent in N; better structure
- Crop residue retention: Retain minimum 30% residue on soil surface after harvest

### 5.2 Vermicompost — Production and Use

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| NPK composition | 1.5–2.5% N; 0.9–1.6% P₂O₅; 1.8–2.4% K₂O |
| C:N Ratio | 15–18:1 (ideal) |
| Application rate | 2–5 t/ha (as basal); can replace 25–30% of chemical N |
| Earthworm species | Eisenia fetida (Red wiggler) — best for composting |
| Production time | 45–60 days for fresh organic waste |

- Source: ICAR-IISS Organic Farming Guidelines; Telangana Agriculture Dept Soil Health Programs
