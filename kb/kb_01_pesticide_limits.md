# KB-01: Pesticide Maximum Residue Limits (MRLs) and Crop-Specific Usage Guidelines
## Region: Telangana, Andhra Pradesh | Regulatory Body: FSSAI / CIB&RC India

---

## SECTION 1: Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 Governing Bodies
- **CIB&RC** (Central Insecticides Board and Registration Committee): Registers all pesticide formulations in India. Authority under the Insecticides Act, 1968.
- **FSSAI** (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India): Sets Maximum Residue Limits (MRLs) for all food commodities under FSS (Contaminants, Toxins and Residues) Regulations, 2011.
- **APEDA**: Enforces MRL compliance for export produce.
- **Telangana State Agriculture Department**: Issues state-level advisories and approved pesticide lists per crop.

### 1.2 Key Regulatory Documents
- Insecticides Act, 1968 and Insecticides Rules, 1971
- FSS (Contaminants, Toxins and Residues) Regulations, 2011 (amended 2020)
- CIB&RC Registered Pesticide Database (https://cibrc.nic.in)
- FSSAI MRL Database (https://fssai.gov.in/upload/uploadfiles/files/Compendium_Pesticide_Residues.pdf)

## SECTION 3: Banned and Restricted Pesticides (India)

### 3.1 Completely Banned in India (Selected List)
These must never be recommended under any circumstance.

| Pesticide | Ban Order | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Endosulfan | Supreme Court 2011; Stockholm Convention 2012 | Persistent organic pollutant, endocrine disruptor |
| Methyl Parathion | CIB&RC 1982 | Extremely high mammalian toxicity |
| DDT | Restricted 1989; production banned | POP, bioaccumulation |
| Aldrin / Dieldrin / Heptachlor | CIB&RC | Persistent organic pollutants |
| Chlordane | CIB&RC | POP |
| Monocrotophos (on vegetables) | CIB&RC | High mammalian toxicity on open crops |
| Trifluralin | Govt notification 2020 | Environmental persistence |

- Sodium Cyanide (rodenticide — licensed operators)

## SECTION 4: Safe Pesticide Use Practices

### 4.1 PHI Compliance
- Never harvest before the PHI has elapsed.
- PHI must be counted from the date of the LAST application, not the first.
- If multiple pesticides are applied, use the longest PHI among all applied pesticides.

### 4.2 Dilution and Dosage Reference
- Always use calibrated measuring equipment.
- Sprayer calibration: 500 litres of water per hectare is standard reference volume for knapsack sprayer in field crops.
- Do not increase dosage beyond label recommendation — this does not increase efficacy and increases residue risk.

### 4.3 Resistance Management
- Rotate insecticide classes: do not apply two consecutive sprays from the same MoA group.
- Recommended rotation for Bollworm in Cotton:
  - Spray 1: Diamide (Chlorantraniliprole)
  - Spray 2: Spinosyn (Spinosad)
  - Spray 3: Avermectin (Emamectin Benzoate)
  - Do not repeat same class within a season.
- For BPH in Paddy: Rotate Neonicotinoids → Pymetrozine → Buprofezin.

### 4.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Minimum PPE: Rubber gloves, goggles, cotton mask, full-sleeve clothing, rubber boots.
- Class I (Extremely Hazardous) pesticides require full respirator and chemical-resistant suit.
- Never spray without PPE. Never eat, drink, or smoke during spraying.
