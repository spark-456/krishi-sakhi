- Farmers in these zones must practice deficit irrigation and rainwater harvesting.

- Source: CGWB (Central Ground Water Board) Telangana District Reports 2022; Telangana Irrigation Department; Mission Kakatiya Reports

## SECTION 2: Crop-wise Irrigation Requirements

### 2.1 Paddy — Oryza sativa

Paddy is the highest water-consuming crop. Traditional flood irrigation uses 1200–2000 mm per crop. Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD) can reduce this by 30–40%.

#### Water Requirement by Growth Stage
| Growth Stage | Duration (Days) | Water Requirement (mm) | Critical? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nursery | 25 | 150–200 | Yes |
| Land preparation + puddling | 7–10 | 200–300 | Yes |
| Transplanting to tillering | 0–30 DAT | 250–300 | Yes (standing water 2–5 cm) |
| Tillering to panicle initiation | 30–60 DAT | 200–250 | Critical (Blast risk if stress) |
| Panicle initiation to heading | 60–80 DAT | 150–200 | Most critical — no stress |
| Heading to grain filling | 80–100 DAT | 100–150 | High — spikelet sterility risk |
| Grain filling to maturity | 100–125 DAT | 50–75 | Moderate — drain 10 days before harvest |
| **Total (Traditional Flood)** | | **1200–1800 mm** | |
| **Total (AWD method)** | | **750–1000 mm** | 30–40% water saving |

#### Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD) Protocol
1. Install a simple PVC pipe (10 cm diameter) with holes at 10 cm intervals, 30 cm deep into soil.
2. Irrigate normally until 5 cm standing water.
3. Allow water to decline. Check pipe water level daily.
4. Re-irrigate when water level in pipe drops to 15 cm below soil surface (non-critical stages) or 5 cm (critical: PI to heading).
5. Never let soil crack during PI to heading stage.

**AWD is NOT recommended during:**
- Panicle initiation (5 days before to 10 days after)
- Heading stage
- Grain filling first 2 weeks

- Source: IRRI AWD Protocol; ICAR-CRRI Water Management Guidelines; PJTSAU Paddy Agronomy Manual

### 2.2 Cotton — Gossypium hirsutum

#### Water Requirement and Irrigation Schedule
| Growth Stage | Duration (Days from Sowing) | Critical? | Irrigation Interval (Drip) | Irrigation Interval (Furrow) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Germination–Establishment | 0–21 | Yes | Daily @ 3–4 mm/day | One pre-sowing + one at 15 DAS |
| Seedling–Early square | 21–60 | Moderate | Every 2 days | Every 10–14 days |
| Square initiation–Flowering | 60–90 | High | Daily @ 4–5 mm/day | Every 7–10 days |
| Boll formation | 90–120 | Critical | Daily @ 5–6 mm/day | Every 7 days |
| Boll maturation | 120–150 | Moderate | Every 3 days | Every 12–14 days |
| Boll opening | 150–180 | Low | Withhold (rain > 50 mm risk of boll rot) | Stop 3 weeks before harvest |
| **Total Seasonal Requirement** | | | **650–800 mm (drip)** | **800–1100 mm (furrow)** |

**Critical Stress Points:**
- Moisture stress at squaring (-0.8 MPa) reduces lint yield by 20–30%.
- Moisture stress at boll filling (-1.2 MPa) reduces boll weight and lint %age.
- Waterlogging > 48 hours causes root rot and Fusarium wilt infection.

#### Drip Irrigation for Cotton — Recommended System
- Lateral spacing: 1.5 m (one lateral per row pair)
- Dripper spacing: 30–40 cm
- Dripper flow rate: 4 L/hr
- Operating pressure: 1.0–1.5 kg/cm²
- Water saving vs. flood: 30–40%; yield increase: 15–25%

- Source: ICAR-CICR Cotton Agronomy; Telangana CADA Drip Guidelines; PJTSAU Cotton Production Manual

### 2.3 Groundnut — Arachis hypogaea

| Growth Stage | Days from Sowing | ETc (mm) | Critical? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Germination | 0–15 | 25–35 | Yes — poor germination if dry |
| Vegetative | 15–35 | 60–80 | Moderate |
| Flowering–Pegging | 35–60 | 80–100 | Critical — pegging requires moist surface soil |
| Pod development | 60–90 | 100–120 | Critical — pod fill; most critical stage |
| Pod maturation | 90–110 | 40–60 | Moderate — over-irrigation delays maturity |
| **Total** | | **310–400 mm** | |

**Irrigation Method:**
- Flood (furrow): Irrigate every 10–12 days depending on soil type.
- Sprinkler: Most efficient; 25–30% water saving; better germination uniformity.
- Critical: Peg zone (top 5–10 cm) must not dry out between pegging and pod setting (Days 40–80).

**Telangana Dryland Groundnut Note:**
- In SRZ, groundnut is largely rainfed; conserved moisture from Kharif rains (500–700 mm) usually sufficient if sown at onset.
- One life-saving irrigation at pegging stage dramatically increases pod yield in dry years.

- Source: ICAR-ICRISAT Groundnut Water Management; PJTSAU Groundnut Agronomy

**Critical Rule for Maize:** Never allow drought stress from 7 days before tasseling to 10 days after silking. This window is absolute.

### 2.5 Chickpea — Cicer arietinum (Rabi)

Chickpea is primarily grown under residual soil moisture in Rabi. Excess irrigation is harmful.

| Growth Stage | Days | ETc (mm) | Irrigation Need |
|---|---|---|---|
| Germination | 0–15 | 20–25 | Pre-sowing + one at 10 DAS if very dry |
| Vegetative | 15–40 | 40–60 | Usually no irrigation if soil moisture adequate |
| Flowering | 40–60 | 50–60 | One irrigation if no rain for 3 weeks |
| Pod filling | 60–85 | 60–70 | One irrigation; critical for pod weight |
| Maturity | 85–100 | 20 | Do not irrigate — causes lodging and disease |
| **Total** | | **180–220 mm** | **1–2 irrigations maximum** |

**Critical Warning:** Overwatering chickpea causes Fusarium wilt, Botrytis gray mold, and root rot. Waterlogging even for 12 hours at flowering can cause 100% flower drop.

### 2.6 Tomato — Solanum lycopersicum (Horticulture)

| Growth Stage | Days after Transplanting | ETc (mm/day) | Irrigation (Drip recommended) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Establishment | 0–15 | 2–3 | Daily drip; maintain high frequency |
| Vegetative | 15–40 | 3–4 | Every alternate day or drip |
| Flowering | 40–60 | 4–5 | Critical; even moisture; prevent blossom end rot |
| Fruit set | 60–80 | 5–6 | Most critical; irregular irrigation → blossom end rot |
| Fruit development | 80–110 | 4–5 | Maintain even; sudden irrigation after stress → cracking |
| Maturity + Harvest | 110–140 | 2–3 | Reduce 2 weeks before final harvest |
| **Total per crop** | | **400–550 mm** | |

**Blossom End Rot (BER) and Irrigation:**
BER is a calcium deficiency caused by irregular moisture supply, not lack of calcium. Solution: consistent drip irrigation every 1–2 days.

**Drip System for Tomato:**
- Lateral spacing: 1.5 m (paired row)
- Dripper spacing: 30 cm
- Dripper discharge: 2–4 L/hr
- Fertigation possible through drip (see KB-04)

## SECTION 3: Irrigation Methods — Comparison and Selection

### 3.1 Method Comparison

| Method | Water Use Efficiency | Capital Cost | Suitable Crops | Topography |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Surface Flood | Low (40–50%) | Very low | Paddy, Sugarcane | Flat to gentle slope |
| Border/Furrow | Moderate (50–60%) | Low | Cotton, Maize, Groundnut, Vegetables | Slight slope; row crops |
| Sprinkler | High (70–80%) | Medium | Groundnut, Maize, Vegetables, Wheat | Undulating; irregular |
| Drip (surface) | Very high (85–95%) | High | Cotton, Tomato, Chilli, Sugarcane | Any topography |
| Sub-surface Drip | Highest (90–95%) | Highest | Sugarcane, Cotton | Flat |

### 3.2 Telangana Subsidy Schemes for Micro-Irrigation
- **PM-KUSUM** and **Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)**: Drip and sprinkler installation subsidy.
- Telangana Micro Irrigation Scheme: SC/ST farmers — 100% subsidy; General farmers — 50–75%.
- Contact: Telangana Horticultural Department / MGNF (Micro Irrigation) offices.
- Source: PMKSY Guidelines 2022; Telangana Micro Irrigation Implementation Agency

## SECTION 4: Soil Moisture Management

### 4.1 Field Capacity and Wilting Point by Soil Type (Telangana)

| Soil Type | Field Capacity (% vol.) | Permanent Wilting Point (% vol.) | Available Water (mm/m depth) | Districts |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Black cotton (Vertisol) | 38–42 | 20–22 | 180–200 | Warangal, Karimnagar, Adilabad |
| Red loam | 28–32 | 12–15 | 130–170 | NTZ, CTZ mixed |
| Red sandy | 18–22 | 8–10 | 80–100 | SRZ, Mahbubnagar |
| Alluvial | 30–35 | 14–16 | 150–190 | River basin areas |

**Irrigation Trigger Rule:**
Irrigate when soil moisture reaches 50% of available water capacity (AWC). Do not wait for wilting signs — crop stress begins before visible wilting in most crops.

### 4.2 Simple Field Methods to Check Soil Moisture

| Method | How To | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Feel method | Take soil from 15 cm depth; press in palm | Ribbons easily = moist; crumbles = dry; no change = very dry |
| Tensiometer | Install at 15 cm and 30 cm depth | < 20 kPa = wet; 20–60 kPa = ideal; > 70 kPa = irrigate |
| Soil probe / auger | Examine color and texture of soil core | Dark = moist; pale = drying; white deposits = saline |

## SECTION 5: Fertigation Through Drip — Basic Guidelines

| Nutrient Stage | Paddy (Not Drip) | Cotton (Drip) | Tomato (Drip) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Establishment (0–30 days) | Basal N+P+K; Urea top dress | 19-19-19 Water Soluble Fertiliser @ 5 kg/ha/week | 20-20-20 WSF @ 3 kg/ha/week |
| Vegetative (30–60 days) | Urea split application | MOP + Urea | 12-61-0 (MAP) + CAN |
| Flowering | — | 0-52-34 (MKP) | 0-52-34 |
| Fruit development | — | CaNO3 (Calcium Nitrate) | CaNO3; MKP |
| Maturity | — | MOP for boll fill | KNO3 |

**Fertigation Rules:**
- Never apply fertiliser for first 15–20 minutes of irrigation cycle; flush drip lines for last 15 minutes after fertigation.
- pH of irrigation water must be 6.0–7.0 for maximum nutrient availability.
- Test irrigation water quality: EC < 0.75 dS/m ideal; > 2.0 dS/m causes crop damage.

- Source: Telangana Horticulture Dept Fertigation Guidelines; ICAR-NRC on Plant Biotechnology; PJTSAU Agronomy Division

## SECTION 6: Irrigation Water Quality Guidelines

| Parameter | Safe Range | Caution Range | Hazard Range | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| EC (dS/m) | < 0.75 | 0.75–3.0 | > 3.0 | Salinity stress |
| pH | 6.5–8.0 | 8.0–8.5 | > 8.5 or < 5.5 | Nutrient availability |
| SAR (Sodium Adsorption Ratio) | < 10 | 10–18 | > 18 | Soil structure damage |
| Boron (mg/L) | < 0.5 | 0.5–2.0 | > 2.0 | Boron toxicity |
| Chloride (mg/L) | < 70 | 70–355 | > 355 | Chloride toxicity |
| Bicarbonate (meq/L) | < 1.5 | 1.5–8.5 | > 8.5 | Calcium precipitation in drip |
| Iron (mg/L) | < 0.1 | 0.1–1.5 | > 1.5 | Drip emitter clogging |

**Groundwater Quality Issue — Telangana:**
- Fluoride levels > 1.5 mg/L in Nalgonda, Medak, Mahbubnagar: toxic to humans; mildly tolerated by crops.
- High EC borewell water common in SRZ: blend with rainwater harvested in farm ponds before use.
- Source: CGWB Groundwater Quality Reports; BIS Water Quality Standards for Irrigation
