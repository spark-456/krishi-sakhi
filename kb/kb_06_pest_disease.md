# KB-06: Pest and Disease Identification and Management — Telangana
## Region: Telangana | All Major Crops

---

## SECTION 1: Paddy Pests and Diseases

### 1.1 Major Insect Pests of Paddy

#### 1.1.1 Brown Plant Hopper (BPH) — Nilaparvata lugens

**Identification:**
- Small brown insect 2–4 mm; lives and feeds at base of plant.
- Feeding causes "hopperburn" — circular yellowing patches in field that turn brown and die.
- Both adults (winged and wingless) and nymphs suck phloem sap.
- Outbreak risk: Dense plantings, high N, continuous flooding, resistant variety breakdown.

**Threshold:** Spray when > 10 nymphs + adults per hill or when yellowing observed.

**Management:**
| Priority | Method | Product/Details |
|---|---|---|
| Cultural | Plant resistant varieties | MTU-1010, DRR Dhan 42 |
| Chemical (first choice) | Buprofezin 25 SC | 625 mL/ha; targets nymphs |
| Chemical (second choice) | Pymetrozine 50 WG | 300 g/ha; unique MoA |
| Chemical (third choice) | Triflumezopyrim 10 SC | 300 mL/ha; new diamide |
| Biological | Nymphal parasitoid — Anagrus nilaparvatae | Conserve by reducing broad-spectrum sprays |

**DO NOT USE:** Imidacloprid, Thiamethoxam, Acetamiprid for BPH — documented resistance in Telangana; resurgence risk.

#### 1.1.2 Yellow Stem Borer — Scirpophaga incertulas

**Identification:**
- White moth lays egg masses on leaves.
- Larvae bore into stem; "Dead Heart" at tillering (dead central tiller); "White Ear" at panicle stage.
- White ear = 100% loss of that panicle.

**Threshold:** 1 egg mass per hill OR 5% dead hearts.

**Management:**
| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| Remove and destroy egg masses | Manual scouting; remove yellow egg mass |
| Cartap Hydrochloride 4G | 18.75 kg/ha (granule broadcast in standing water) |
| Chlorantraniliprole 0.4G | 10 kg/ha (granule) — preferred, lower dose |
| Chlorpyrifos 20 EC + Cypermethrin | 1 L/ha foliar; for adult moth flush |
| Trichogranna japonicum (egg parasitoid) | Release 1.5 lakh cards/ha weekly for 3–4 weeks |

#### 1.1.3 Gall Midge — Orseolia oryzae

**Identification:**
- Maggot tunnels into central shoot forming a "Silver Shoot" (tubular leaf — no blade, looks like onion shoot).
- Not recoverable — affected shoot is lost permanently.
- Peak damage: 4–8 weeks after transplanting.

**Management:**
- Resistant varieties: Surekha, Shakti; avoid susceptible varieties in endemic zones.
- Imidacloprid 17.8 SL — soil drench at nursery (carbofuran is alternative but toxic).
- Avoid early transplanting (before July 1) in endemic zones.
- Source: ICAR-CRRI Pest Management; PJTSAU IPM Guidelines

### 1.2 Major Diseases of Paddy

#### 1.2.1 Blast — Pyricularia oryzae (Magnaporthe oryzae)

**Identification:**
- Leaf blast: Diamond/spindle-shaped lesions with gray center and brown border.
- Neck blast: Black rotting at panicle neck — leads to white head (different from white ear of stem borer in that affected panicle is hollow).
- Occurs in cool (20–28°C), humid (> 90% RH), overcast conditions with wind.
- Severe on high N-applied plots.

**Threshold:** 1–2% leaf area affected = preventive spray. Neck blast: spray at 5–10% heading (panicle emergence).

**Management:**
| Stage | Product | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Leaf blast (early) | Tricyclazole 75 WP | 0.6 g/L (600 g/ha) |
| Leaf blast | Isoprothiolane 40 EC | 1.5 mL/L (1.5 L/ha) |
| Neck blast prevention | Propiconazole 25 EC | 0.1% (1 mL/L) at heading |
| Leaf + neck blast | Azoxystrobin 23 SC | 1 mL/L |

**Cultural:** Avoid excess urea; maintain continuous thin flooding; plant resistant varieties.

#### 1.2.2 Sheath Blight — Rhizoctonia solani

**Identification:**
- Oval to irregular water-soaked lesions on leaf sheath near water line; later gray-green with brown margin.
- Moves up the plant as season progresses.
- Worse in dense plantings; warm (28–32°C) humid weather.

**Management:**
- Trifloxystrobin + Tebuconazole 75 WG @ 0.4 g/L
- Hexaconazole 5 SC @ 1 mL/L
- Reduce plant density: 3–4 seedlings per hill (not 6–8).

#### 1.2.3 Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB) — Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae

**Identification:**
- Water-soaked margins of leaves turning yellow then straw-colored drying from tip.
- Bacterial ooze (milky drops) on cut surface placed in water.
- Kresek (wilt) phase — entire plant wilts in young crops.

**Management:**
- NO effective chemical control — use resistant varieties.
- Remove infected plants; drain water; avoid excessive N.
- Streptocycline spray: limited use; not recommended as routine.

## SECTION 2: Cotton Pests and Diseases

### 2.1 Major Insect Pests of Cotton

#### 2.1.1 American Bollworm — Helicoverpa armigera

**Identification:**
- Young larvae pale green; older larvae dark with lateral stripes (highly variable in color).
- Feeds on squares (flower buds), flowers, and bolls.
- Entry hole in boll; frass (excreta) outside.
- Most damaging: boll stage (60–120 DAS).

**Economic Threshold Level (ETL):** 1–2 eggs or larvae per plant.

**Management (Resistance Management Critical):**
| Spray No. | Product | MoA Group |
|---|---|---|
| 1st (early boll stage) | Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.3 mL/L | Diamide (Group 28) |
| 2nd (7–10 days later) | Spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 mL/L | Spinosyn (Group 5) |
| 3rd (if needed) | Emamectin Benzoate 5 SG @ 0.4 g/L | Avermectin (Group 6) |
| 4th (if needed) | Indoxacarb 14.5 SC @ 0.7 mL/L | Oxadiazine (Group 22) |

**Do NOT use:**
- Cypermethrin alone for bollworm — high resistance.
- Quinalphos, Endosulfan — banned.
- HaNPV (Helicoverpa NPV): 250 LE/ha — spray evening; UV degrades virus.

#### 2.1.2 Pink Bollworm — Pectinophora gossypiella

**Identification:**
- Pink larvae inside bolls; entry hole sealed.
- Internal feeding; rosette flower (failed boll opening).
- Earlier in season than American bollworm; peaks in August–September.

**Management:**
- Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.3 mL/L (most effective)
- Pheromone trap monitoring: place traps at 20/ha; > 8 moths/trap/night = spray.
- Early harvest (don't leave bolls on plant) reduces carryover.

#### 2.1.3 Whitefly — Bemisia tabaci (Silverleaf Whitefly)

**Identification:**
- Tiny white-winged insects under leaves; honeydew and sooty mold on upper surface.
- Transmits Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCuV) — irreversible damage.
- Peak infestation: September–November; dry conditions.

**ETL:** 6–8 nymphs per leaf (3 middle leaves, 3 plants).

**Management:**
| Method | Product | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Neonicotinoid (early) | Thiamethoxam 25 WG | 0.2 g/L (systemic via soil drench at sowing) |
| Neonicotinoid (spray) | Acetamiprid 20 SP | 0.2 g/L |
| Non-neonicotinoid | Diafenthiuron 50 WP | 0.1% |
| IGR | Pyriproxyfen 10 EC | 0.5 mL/L |
| Biological | Beauveria bassiana | 5 g/L (spray under leaves evening) |

**Note:** Rotate MoA strictly — Bemisia tabaci has highest number of documented insecticide resistance cases worldwide.

#### 2.1.4 Thrips — Thrips tabaci, Scirtothrips dorsalis

**Identification:**
- Minute insects (1.5 mm) causing silvery/bronze discoloration on leaves.
- Curling, crinkling of young leaves; stippled appearance.
- Transmits Tomato Spotted Wilt Virus.
- Seedling stage to 45 DAS most critical.

**Management:**
- Imidacloprid 17.8 SL — soil drench at sowing (seed treatment): most effective.
- Foliar: Spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 mL/L or Acetamiprid @ 0.2 g/L.
- Avoid Pyrethroid sprays — flares thrips and mite populations.

### 2.2 Cotton Diseases

#### 2.2.1 Fusarium Wilt — Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. vasinfectum

**Identification:**
- Yellowing of lower leaves; browning of vascular tissue (cut stem cross-section).
- Plants wilt; eventually die.
- Associated with nematode infections.

**Management:**
- Use resistant hybrids.
- Trichoderma viride 4 kg/ha soil incorporation before sowing.
- Drench with Carbendazim 0.1% at early wilt symptoms.
- No recovery possible once established.

#### 2.2.2 Root Rot — Rhizoctonia bataticola, Macrophomina phaseolina

**Identification:**
- "Seedling blight" in early stage; roots turn dark brown/black.
- Gray wood rot in mature plants under drought stress.

**Management:**
- Avoid waterlogging.
- Seed treatment: Thiram + Carbendazim (4+2 g/kg seed).
- Reduce drought stress: maintain soil moisture.

## SECTION 3: Groundnut Pests and Diseases

### 3.1 Pests

#### 3.1.1 Tobacco Caterpillar — Spodoptera litura

**Identification:**
- Young larvae gregarious (group feeding); older larvae solitary.
- Green to dark brown with triangular spots on abdomen.
- Complete defoliation of groundnut possible in severe outbreak.

**ETL:** 5 egg masses per 10 m row.

**Management:**
- SpNPV (Spodoptera NPV) @ 250 LE/ha — spray evening; 3–4 days lag time.
- Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.3 mL/L.
- Emamectin Benzoate 5 SG @ 0.4 g/L.
- Pheromone traps: Spodoptera lure — 20 traps/ha for monitoring.

#### 3.1.2 Leaf Miner — Aproaerema modicella

**Identification:**
- Blister mines on leaflets; mines merge; leaves dry.
- Peak infestation: pod-setting stage; drought conditions worse.

**Management:**
- Monocrotophos 36 SL @ 1.5 mL/L (registered; use as last resort).
- Quinalphos 25 EC @ 2 mL/L.
- Spray when mining > 10% leaflets affected.

### 3.2 Diseases

#### 3.2.1 Tikka (Early and Late Leaf Spot) — Cercospora arachidicola / Cercosporidium personatum

**Identification:**
- Early leaf spot: circular brown spots with yellow halo; appears at 30–40 DAS.
- Late leaf spot: darker, black-brown spots; appears at 50–70 DAS; no halo.
- Causes premature defoliation; 30–50% yield loss if uncontrolled.

**Management:**
| Product | Dose | Interval |
|---|---|---|
| Mancozeb 75 WP | 2.5 g/L | 10–14 day interval; start at 30 DAS |
| Chlorothalonil 75 WP | 2 g/L | 10–14 days |
| Propiconazole 25 EC | 1 mL/L | 21 days; DMI fungicide |
| Tebuconazole 25.9 EC | 1 mL/L | 21 days |

#### 3.2.2 Rust — Puccinia arachidis

**Identification:**
- Orange-red uredosori (pustules) on underside of leaflets.
- Appears after 50 DAS; often co-infection with late leaf spot.
- Rapid spread under humid conditions.

**Management:**
- Propiconazole 25 EC or Tebuconazole 25.9 EC.
- First spray at rust onset; repeat at 15–21 days.

## SECTION 4: Tomato Pests and Diseases

### 4.1 Pests

#### 4.1.1 Fruit Borer — Helicoverpa armigera

**Identification:**
- Entry holes in fruit; larvae inside fruit; frass outside hole.
- Damage from flower bud stage onwards.
- Same pest as cotton bollworm — management similar.

**ETL:** 1 egg/plant or 5% flowers with damage.

**Management:**
- HaNPV @ 250 LE/ha (spray evening; 3–4 day lag).
- Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.3 mL/L (best).
- Emamectin Benzoate 5 SG @ 0.4 g/L.
- Spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 mL/L.

#### 4.1.2 Leaf Miner — Liriomyza trifolii

**Identification:**
- Serpentine white mines in leaves; adult fly lays eggs in leaf tissue.
- Cosmetic damage at low levels; heavy infestation causes premature leaf drop.

**Management:**
- Abamectin 1.9 EC @ 0.5 mL/L (specific; most effective).
- Avoid broad-spectrum pyrethroids — kill natural enemies.

### 4.2 Diseases

#### 4.2.1 Late Blight — Phytophthora infestans

**Identification:**
- Water-soaked lesions on leaves; white sporangiophore growth on underside.
- Rapid browning and death of tissue.
- Fruit: brown leathery rot.
- Conditions: Cool (15–22°C), wet, foggy weather. Severe in October–December rabi season.

**Management:**
- Cymoxanil + Mancozeb 72 WP @ 3 g/L.
- Metalaxyl + Mancozeb 72 WP @ 3 g/L (systemic).
- Propiconazole 25 EC @ 1 mL/L.
- Preventive copper oxychloride @ 3 g/L when conditions favorable.
- Spray every 7 days during outbreak conditions.

#### 4.2.2 Bacterial Wilt — Ralstonia solanacearum

**Identification:**
- Sudden complete wilting of entire plant; appears healthy in early morning; wilts by afternoon.
- Vascular discoloration (brown) visible on stem cross-section.
- Milky bacterial ooze when stem placed in water.

**Management:**
- NO effective chemical cure.
- Remove and destroy infected plants + surrounding soil.
- Soil solarization pre-planting: 45 days wet soil covered with clear polythene.
- Resistant rootstocks: Grafting onto Solanum torvum or S. sisymbriifolium rootstock.
- 4-year rotation out of Solanaceous crops.

## SECTION 6: General IPM Principles

### 6.1 Pest Monitoring Protocol
1. Walk diagonally across the field; sample 5 plants at each of 5 locations (25 plants total).
2. Record: pest count/plant, growth stage, crop variety.
3. Compare with ETL before making spray decision.
4. Never spray on fixed schedule without scouting.

### 6.2 Natural Enemy Conservation
- Spiders: Major predators of BPH, whitefly. Preserved by avoiding Pyrethroids and Neonicotinoids.
- Chrysoperla (Green Lacewing): Predator of eggs and small larvae. Release 50,000 eggs/ha.
- Trichogramma (egg parasitoid): Target bollworm eggs. Release 1.5 lakh/ha/week.
- Coccinellid (Ladybird beetle): Predator of aphids and mealybugs.
- Preserve by: minimizing broad-spectrum sprays, using biopesticides, establishing refugia (border rows of non-Bt crop).

### 6.3 Spray Technique Quality
- Correct nozzle: Flat fan nozzle for foliar; hollow cone for BPH (directed at base).
- Spray volume: 500 L/ha minimum; 750 L/ha for heavy canopy crops.
- Spray time: Early morning or evening (5–8 AM; 5–7 PM) — wind minimal; temperature lower; beneficial insects inactive.
- Adjuvant: Add sticker/spreader at 1 mL/L when spraying waxy leaf crops or during high temperature.
